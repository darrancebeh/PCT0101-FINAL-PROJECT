Foods and beverages

Foods

  1. Smørrebrød/smør og brød

    2. Pronounciation:

    Smørrebrød [ˈsmɶɐ̯ˌpʁœðˀ], also name as 'butter and bread' in English is a traditional open-faced sandwich in the cuisines of Denmark.

    The ingredients of Smørrebrød:

    • Rugbrød (a dense, dark brown bread)
    • Cold cuts
    • Meat/fish
    • Thinly sliced cheese
    • Cucumber/tomatoes/red onions
    • Boiled eggs

  2. Aebleflæsk

    3. Pronounciation:

    Aebleflæsk [ˈeːpləˌflesk] is a traditional Danish dish that consisted of cured or salted pork belly/flæsk fried with apple chopped onion mix, thyme and sugar. This dish is very popular in Funen.

      Steps on making Aebleflæsk:
    1. Pork belly is fried first in skillet.
    2. Fried pork belly is moved to a plate, tart apple is added into skillet.
    3. Add spices and sugar into the skillet, mix together.
    4. When the apple is soften, serve it on the plate.
    5. Mixed it with rugbrød and enjoy it!

    P.S.: It's best to enjoy Aebleflæsk with beer and snaps!


Beverages

  1. Cocio

    2. Pronounciation:

    Cocio is a chocolate milk drink produced in Esberj, Denmark. This drink is available all across the world, including Scandinavia, US (New England Region), Spain, United Kingdom, Philippines, Spain, Netherlands and Poland.


  2. Cherry Heering

    3. Alert! Don't drive after drinking alcohol. Protect yourself and others lives!

    Pronounciation:

    Cherry Heering is a Danish liqueur flavoured with cherries. It usually referred as Peter Heering or Cherry Heering in cocktail recipes. Cherry Heering is one of the first cherry liqueurs to appear on the market. The liqueur is made with Stevens cherries that are lightly crushed and then macerated in a neutral grain spirit, which is additionally flavored with botanicals. The liqueur has an appealing garnet color, while the palate is dominated by cherry jam flavors, along with hints of spices and subtle tartness.
